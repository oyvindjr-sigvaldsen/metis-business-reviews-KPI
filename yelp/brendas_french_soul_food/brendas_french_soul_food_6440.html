I haven't been here in a couple months, but wanted to leave a review anyway. One of my favorite restaurants in SF. The line sucks (think a couple hours on the weekent -- plan to go to Peet's a couple blocks away), but it's worth it. The food is hearty and tempting; even when I mean to go healthy, I still end up getting shrimp and grits or beignets (or both). The crawfish beignets are life changing. I really thought it was a gross idea at first, and I'm a sweet lady, but these totally blow my mind every time. I've had weekends where I've gone back more than once...

I love the overall vibe of the place, but I'm not as taken with it as nearly everyone I know. The food is solidly good--well-executed, well-portioned, at an adequate price point for the quality. I would not wait an hour and a half for it, as tends to be the case for weekend brunch wait times. While delicious, the food does not evoke in me the warm fuzziness of creole perfection that seems to drive the foodie world nuts right now. The Sloppy Josephine is the best veggie sloppy joe I've had. The beignets are all right. I wouldn't get them again now that I've had the experience. The service is adequate, but the waits are unbelievable. I don't think you can make reservations. The maitre d' has you write your name on a board when you arrive, and just staring at the list of ten people in front of you for an hour and a half is depressing. It's a cute date place if you have a lot of time to kill and want a relaxed brunch atmosphere with high quality food. If you want to impress someone (e.g., new flame, parent, friend from out of town), take her here.

    I love the overall vibe of the place, but I'm not as taken with it as nearly everyone I know. The food is solidly good--well-executed, well-portioned, at an adequate price point for the quality. I would not wait an hour and a half for it, as tends to be the case for weekend brunch wait times. While delicious, the food does not evoke in me the warm fuzziness of creole perfection that seems to drive the foodie world nuts right now.

    The Sloppy Josephine is the best veggie sloppy joe I've had. The beignets are all right. I wouldn't get them again now that I've had the experience. They are just a huge mess of dough and I've had better. I've had a few other things that were great: french toast, one of the omelettes, pancakes. I'd like to try the grits at some point.

    The service is adequate, but the waits are unbelievable. I don't think you can make reservations. The maitre d' has you write your name on a board when you arrive, and just staring at the list of ten people in front of you for an hour and a half is depressing.

    It's a cute date place if you have a lot of time to kill and want a relaxed brunch atmosphere with high quality food. If you want to impress someone (e.g., new flame, parent, friend from out of town), take her here.
