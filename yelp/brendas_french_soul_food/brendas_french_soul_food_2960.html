(I've been to Cafe du Monde in NOLA, so I can tell you what a true beignet is supposed to be like.) The consistency was more reminiscent of a savory donut or a calzone... which is perfectly fine and delicious, but not a beignet. Anyhow, it was still good but somewhat too doughy. (See next paragraph.) The shrimp and grits also came highly recommended. These were the best grits I've ever had -- and the shrimps were seasoned and cooked to perfection. Unfortunately, there was a bit too much cheese on the grits for my taste. I problem solved by dipping my beignet dough into the cheesy grits -- yummmm. Rich, carbalicious gluttony. After eating 85% of my half of this meal, I felt unpleasantly stuffed and waved the white flag. (My NY's resolution is to set better limits in life. Yay me.) The drinks were perfect for ending our meal. The guava mimosa was the perfect ratio of alcohol to guava juice. Not a bad start to brunching in the City.
