    I've been waiting forever to find somebody who hasn't tried out a Tartine morning bun to experience it with me for the first time. Like your first love, you go through a happy 3 month honeymoon period and then it goes down the crap shoot. My love affair with Tartine's morning bun lasted much more like three seconds than 3 months. Here's why.

    The lines moved pretty quick considering there's this urban myth going around that people wait an hour to taste their buns. So when I got to the counter within 20 minutes, I was excited! I really had a hunkering for a double pain au chocolat (i've had a craving for 2 months now-what does a woman have to do to get a chocolate croissant around here!?). But they were out. Almond croissant? Also out. *sigh* Opted for a plain Jane one and a morning bun. I giddily went over to Dolores Park (since the inside was super crowded and I hate crowds) and sunk my teeth into my first taste of the morning bun.

    I had the silly notion that the bun was suppose to be warm and soft on the inside, not to be. Perhaps it was because I went after 2pm on a Sunday (to avoids the lines)? The edges were flaky and crusty with a tad hint of citrus and sugary goodness. Similar to the butterfly cookies you get to the supermarket. It was yum. But as I worked my way into the center, thinking it'd get softer, it didn't. Instead, it got sticky and chewy (not a big fan). My partner in crime liked it and thought it was worth it. I was less impressed.

    The croissant was quite good. Crusty around the outside, soft in the inside. Similar to Bouchon's croissant. But this one was huge and a little heavier. I'm no French croissant expert.  For what its worth, this commoner thought the croissant tasted good. So thumbs up.

    Conclusion: Worth an hour wait? Probably not. Now that I think of it, I didn't get a waif of the gorgeous aromas people boast about. I give Tartine credit though, they do make some pretty mean baked goods. At least city dwellers don't have to go as far as Bouchon in Yountville for good bread and baked goods no longer.
