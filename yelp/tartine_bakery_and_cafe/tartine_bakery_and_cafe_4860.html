Is writing a review of Tartine Bakery required for Yelp membership? It seems like it. I've never had anything here I didn't like. That qualifies for as good as it gets, right? Granted, it's well nigh impossible to find parking around here and the seating situation is a every man for himself ordeal, but that shouldn't take away from the awesome baked goods they pump out here. I guess the great thing about Tartine is that you're meant to take it with you, and you can head over to Dolores Park and eat it there if it's sunny. Otherwise just roll up with someone else driving and do a hit-and-run Tartine run. The croissants are tres bien. The almond croissants are also tres bien. The chocolate croissants, while also being excellent, could do with a little more chocolate inside. It's not like I'm expecting a whole chocolate bar wrapped in buttery, flaky dough, but more than a few morsels would be great. The banana cream tarts here are fantastic. I'd fight a wolverine if it came near me while I was eating one. The gougeres are almost incomparable. The pressed sandwiches will not disappoint. If you're reading this, you're not really deciding whether or not to come here, so what's the dealbreaker? You don't like the Mission? You feel like the feng shui of this bakery is wrong? OK, I don't want to hype up this place too much if this happens to be the only review you read on this place, so my overall evaluation is, "yeah, if you're in the area, go ahead and drop in."

I think key to having an excellent experience at Tartine is to avoid it on the weekends. The line goes pretty quick on weekday afternoons, even right at noon when the sandwiches come out. The jambon royale & gruyere is tasty but the generous slathering of dijon tends to overpower the sandwich. Just request that they go light on the mustard. The pecorino & almond is perfect as is. Crunchy cheesy goodness! I think the only potential gripe with the sandwiches is that they tend to be a little greasy. Just call and reserve a loaf or two so you don't have to worry about showing up after 5 p.m. and have them run out. They use their country bread as the base and variations of it include walnut, sesame seed, olive, or dates and olives. I like the country best for sandwiches and walnut to eat by the slice. Also, freeze the leftovers! The ones with nuts and olives keep better as they keep moisture in. $6 for the plain country loaf and $6.50 for the others.

    How can you possibly review a place after 1,026 others have written before you? The irony is that I was around when this location opened. Oh well. I've never been too much of an early adapter.

    Okay, I once declared that Tartine's ham-and-cheese croissant was the single best food item in the 49 square-miles of this city. Today, I reiterate that claim. Mouth-watering, perfectly baked and flaky croissant filled with Niman Ranch ham and, I believe, gruyere (not sure where from). Everything is as hot and textured as it's meant to be, every time. The ham is slightly juicy, and a bit smoky. But enough of my yappin' -- just try one already.

    I haven't visited a substantial portion of the menu, but I can say that everything I've ever had has been utterly delicious -- morning buns (bits of orange rind round this out), plain croissants (again, perfect flakes), croque moisseurs, almond croissants (frangipane), bread pudding (with seasonal fruit), and, well, I know I'm forgetting a bunch of other things.

    Coffee is by Mr. Espresso, and I don't care what you say (well, I kind of do), it's damn good. Fresh orange and grapefruit juice.

    Okay, the downside (honestly, what keeps it from getting six stars) is the line, made worse by the fact that the place is just way, way too small. Perhaps not their fault, but my solution is to open Bar Tartine during the morning and early afternoon and serve coffee and pastries there. So I sort of feel like I can fault them for the line. But not really. The love is strong.

    Prices are reasonable ($3.25 for aforementioned best food item in the city). Get it to go if you can't stomach the line, take it to nearby Dolores Park, and let life be grand.
