I was jogging with my friend David when it came to me: my ideal day. It would be an entire day spent at Tartine. I would begin with the morning bun and a latte and catch up on three months of unread New Yorkers. When lunch rolled around, I would order one of the hot pressed sandwiches, perhaps the mozzarella and tapenade. Then I might take a walk around Dolores Park to work up my appetite, and return for a mid-afternoon snack of bread pudding. As evening set in, I'd shift to the wine bar. Then I realized it --- this was an achievable dream. I told a half dozen or so friends I'd be at Tartine, and said they should drop by during the day. Yesterday was the day. I began with a large latte, which came in a big white bowl and was made with Mr. Espresso coffee. The morning bun was as good as I'd remembered it --- flaky, buttery pastry with caramelized, crunchy sugar and a hint of orange. My friends Sheila and Chris showed up, with their dog, Simon, then my friend Emily. We sat on a bench outside and talked about how difficult the semester had been. Then we headed to Dolores Park, where Simon ran around, played catch and made friends with some of the other dogs. David showed up, and we went to Pizzeria Delfina for lunch, a small but worthwhile diversion from my all-day Tartine plan. We returned to Tartine after lunch, and met my sister and Kelly. Katie and David both got the bread pudding. There were two choices: one topped with banana, and the other topped with seasonal fruit; yesterday, it was strawberry. The small-sized serving comes in a coffee cup. It's not big, but it's enough, because the pudding, made from the bakery's house made brioche, is so rich. Five o'clock rolled around, and I was getting a little tired. I decided to save the wine bar for another day. But before I left, I picked up a banana cream tart, some macaroons and a few meringues with cocoa nibs and took them over to Emily's. We sat on her sofa and devoured the tart. It was delicious, as everything I have eaten at Tartine has been. The bananas are lightly blanketed in a caramel sauce, and tucked into a flaky pastry shell lined with dark chocolate. This whole concoction is topped with freshly whipped cream and shaved chocolate. Steven showed up a little later, and we gave him some of the macaroons and meringues. I still had a couple meringues left over, so I ate one for breakfast this morning. Kelly introduced me to them, and I am glad she did. The meringue is crunchy and sweet on the outside, and a bit sticky and gooey on the inside. Its sweetness is cut with the earthiness and crunch of the cacao nibs. I never did get to any of my New Yorkers, but it was a great day, and a perfect end to the semester.
