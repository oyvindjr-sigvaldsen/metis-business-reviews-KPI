So many different characters have passed through the doors of Gary Danko, each with their own cultural nuances and quirkiness, but there is one thing that brought us all together under one roof: our exemplary taste in food. Gary Danko stands out from other fine dining restaurants because they make it their prerogative to give diners top notch service that may never be experienced else where. From constantly filling our glass with water without asking and keeping our food hot by covering it when we leave for the restroom to ensuring that each course comes out at a constant rate while keeping a smile on their face. I remember at least 5 different faces that served us and each one had a role that was fulfilled beautifully. Along with quality service, Gary Danko provides many complimentary items such as our amuse bouche-anise cured salmon with jicama salad and lotus root that was refreshing and successful in cleansing our palate.     When you step into Gary Danko, you enter a perfect world where nothing can go wrong, a utopia if you will. In this utopia, everyone is happy from your mysterious dining neighbors and even the alluring men who serve you like royalty. Since the tables are placed close together, it is easy to strike up a conversation with other patrons. A piece of wisdom is always gained from speaking to elders such as the lovely couple from Hawaii who were there on a business trip and an older couple from Newport Beach who perhaps stopped by for dinner after visiting their alma mater UCSF. So many different characters have passed through the doors of Gary Danko, each with their own cultural nuances and quirkiness, but there is one thing that brought us all together under one roof: our exemplary taste in food.

    Gary Danko stands out from other fine dining restaurants because they make it their prerogative to give diners top notch service that may never be experienced else where. From constantly filling our glass with water without asking and keeping our food hot by covering it when we leave for the restroom to ensuring that each course comes out at a constant rate while keeping a smile on their face. I remember at least 5 different faces that served us and each one had a role that was fulfilled beautifully.

    Along with quality service, Gary Danko provides many complimentary items such as our amuse bouche-anise cured salmon with jicama salad and lotus root that was refreshing and successful in cleansing our palate. There were dessert samplers to take home and at the end of our meal, a banana cream cheese breakfast cake with gold wrapping was handed to me while my bf was handed the bill. They also comped a lemon ice box cake with the words "Happy Anniversary" inscribed on the plate in dark chocolate and our charming server graced us with a brief speech to wish us many more anniversaries to come. Lord any woman would love this place.

    Also worth boasting about is their flexible menu where you aren't restricted to ordering the standard appetizer, entree, and dessert combo. In fact, you can get all main entrees if you have a hearty appetite.

    Moving on to the highlights of my 4 course meal: I ordered 2 entrees with lobster and they ended up being my 2 favorites. The lobster risotto was an explosion of flavors on my tongue. Each spoonful had creamy risotto, succulent lobster and tender shimeji mushrooms. I had to stop in between bites to catch my breath. My other lobster dish, roast Maine Lobster, used the lobster at its finest and cooked precisely to my liking. The chef did not make the mistake of even slightly overcooking it where the texture would be chewy and rubbery. Partly translucent, the lobster had the perfect crisp with every bite. The quail entree I ordered was stuffed with foie gras, mushroom and quinoa. Tender, juicy and flavorful, the quail out shined any other quail entree I've tasted. Lastly, the chocolate souffle was good-light and airy on the tongue but missing that special element to make it spectacular. I sampled my bf's entrees and have to say the coconut Thai curry left me salivating with just one bite. The intense aroma of kaffir lime leaves that dominated this dish would have to be fully experienced by me on another occasion.

    When a restaurant leaves little room for error, it's no surprise they are considered the best restaurant in San Francisco. Gary Danko leaves you coming back for more because you won't find impeccable food and service anywhere else in the bay area. With 2500+ reviews and 4.5 stars, I'm sure many yelpers would echo this sentiment and like me, anticipate the next time they can come back for that one dish they missed out on.
