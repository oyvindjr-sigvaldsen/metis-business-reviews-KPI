So onto my review of each course: Crispy Farm Egg with White Polenta: The egg was definitely not your chicken-laid egg and had the right amount of crispyness and fresh taste. The accents on the side comboed with the egg to make a great starter. Risotto with Lobster: My experience with risotto is that it just blends into some noddle/rice glob, but not so here. I could taste every grain and the lobster was distinguishable and added the extra flair needed to the already delicious risotto. Roast Maine Lobster with Potato Pure: Best Potatoes ever! And this coming from a connoisseur of potatoes. So when the potatoes are that good, you know the main part, the lobster was more than succulent. Quail Stuffed with Foie Gras: No deboning left for the petty diner to handle here since the foie gras was adequately stuffed inside and only the drumstick was boned. I could definitely taste the fattening in the foie gras, but that did not stop me. Juniper Crusted Venison: Venison definitely tasted gamey here, but man, the chestnu gnocchi gave us a good distraction in the form of a delectable treat. Seared Filet of Beef: I thought the venison would give a contrast to the taste of this beef, so it was nice experiencing the classic well-cooked cow. It wasn't sizzling off the plate with heart-stopping butter like Ruth's Chris, but it had the same level of crispyness on the outside and medium-rare goodness of the venison so no complaints here. Braised Lamb Shoulder and Herb Crusted Loin: When I tasted this dish, I could only think middle east meets further East since it was split right down the middle between the shoulder and the loin. The shoulder tasted like a middle eastern dish that was perfectly meaty with couscous on the side. Not to be stereotypical, but hey, if I stepped into a middle eastern restaurant, that's what I would probably get. But wait, there was a twist in this foray into dessert world. Our souffle was brought out with the Louisiana butter cake and ice cream, and I was marveling at how tasty this unexpected surprise looked, I had to mention that I had ordered the creme brulee.     I normally only write a review for things I feel passionate about, and Gary Danko's is very well-deserved. From the food to the service, everything is top-notch. But before we could decide, we were served a vietnamese beef salad dish that according to Linda, was sooo cute and equally tasty. I had to concur, no homo...

    So onto my review of each course:

    Crispy Farm Egg with White Polenta: The egg was definitely not your chicken-laid egg and had the right amount of crispyness and fresh taste. The accents on the side comboed with the egg to make a great starter.
    Risotto with Lobster: My experience with risotto is that it just blends into some noddle/rice glob, but not so here. I could taste every grain and the lobster was distinguishable and added the extra flair needed to the already delicious risotto.
    Roast Maine Lobster with Potato Pure: Best Potatoes ever! And this coming from a connoisseur of potatoes. So when the potatoes are that good, you know the main part, the lobster was more than succulent. It wasn't over-buttered like at all the cheap places and it just tasted fresh.
    Pancetta Wrapped Frog Legs: Ok, so we just wanted to say we ate frog legs in ordering this dish and we don't even like lentils but yeah, it's just fancy tasting chicken so let's move on.
    Quail Stuffed with Foie Gras: No deboning left for the petty diner to handle here since the foie gras was adequately stuffed inside and only the drumstick was boned. I'm used to greasy asian-fried quail so this was a nice departure as it was still quite crispy and not overly seasoned. I could definitely taste the fattening in the foie gras, but that did not stop me.
    Juniper Crusted Venison: Venison definitely tasted gamey here, but man, the chestnu gnocchi gave us a good distraction in the form of a delectable treat.
    Seared Filet of Beef: I thought the venison would give a contrast to the taste of this beef, so it was nice experiencing the classic well-cooked cow. It wasn't sizzling off the plate with heart-stopping butter like Ruth's Chris, but it had the same level of crispyness on the outside and medium-rare goodness of the venison so no complaints here.
    Braised Lamb Shoulder and Herb Crusted Loin: When I tasted this dish, I could only think middle east meets further East since it was split right down the middle between the shoulder and the loin. The shoulder tasted like a middle eastern dish that was perfectly meaty with couscous on the side. Not to be stereotypical, but hey, if I stepped into a middle eastern restaurant, that's what I would probably get. Moving onto the herb crusted loan which had that nice level of tangy spicyness that reminded me of a thai method of cooking. Definitely, a smooth transition between the two styles of cooking.

    Now onto my favorite part, the desserts! Since I have sweet teeth, not just tooth cuz I got multiple. We had to get the chocolate souffle, since every other picture on yelp was souffle, but I couldn't make up my mind on the other one so I just settled on the creme brulee trio. The souffle was super moist and airy and the dripping sauces made it that much more tasty. I tried the traditional creme brulee before tackling the more exotic squash and pumpkin, I think it was. The cream was definitely thicker than what I was used to and Yes, that is what she said. Anyways, I couldn't get past the taste of the squash so I left that as unfinished but I devoured the pumpkin and it had the same thick consistency as the others. But wait, there was a twist in this foray into dessert world. Our souffle was brought out with the Louisiana butter cake and ice cream, and I was marveling at how tasty this unexpected surprise looked, I had to mention that I had ordered the creme brulee. Naturally, this being Gary Danko's the server immediately apologized and said this dessert would be comped and the creme brulee was brought out minutes later. Even more gratifying was that the birthday dessert with the sampler tray of treats was brought out so Linda could make her special wish. I guess my wish had already come true as we had a dessert orgy with all four desserts on the table. Needless to say, the buttercake and ice cream was the underdog winner of the dessert race as it gave me that warm, homey feel. Sadly, we had to box the birthday dessert, but that will be a nice addition to our gifted breakfast bun as we reminisce about the awesomeness that was Gary Danko's.
