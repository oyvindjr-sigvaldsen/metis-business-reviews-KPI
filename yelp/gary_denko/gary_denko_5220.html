    Update: More than a year later

    I went to Gary Danko last night for my second visit (first visit was more than a year ago).  What a disappointment not only in food quality but also service.

    In short: Gary Danko seems to have become too complacent with his success and/or tired of making sure everything's perfect, which is what people should expect of Gary Danko.  The service is sloppier and so is the food.  Gary Danko shouldn't present a dish to a guest unless and until he is quite certain that the guest will be blown away by it.  He was able to accomplish this a year ago, but not now.  Furthermore, he shouldn't have decided to reduce the portion sizes in some of the dishes, such as the cheese cart (allowing only 4 choices of cheese, as opposed to the former 7).  People notice these things.  They would notice these things especially more of a restaurant of Gary Danko's caliber.  Reduction in product portion is more easily noticeable than a slight increase in price, which would have been the wiser choice because Gary Danko is expensive to begin with and because people are more sympathetic toward price inflation than quantity deflation.

    Food qualiy:
    The sweet potato soup that I had as a complimentary appetizer at the beginning was much better than anything else I ordered.  I ordered the four-course meal:

    Lobster with risotto and rock shrimp: Too salty and the cheese flavors overpowered the lobster and shrimp.  When I had this dish the first time, I remember being blown away.  This time, the dish was a disappointment.

    Scallops: The woman at the table next to me had ordered this same dish as part of her three-course meal and she had four scallops, whereas I had only two.  I didn't appreciate the reduction of my scallops dish by half merely because I had ordered an extra course.

    Beef Tenderloin: This was the worst dish I had.  The beef was certainly tender, but the flavors were quite bland and I certainly wasn't blown away by this dish the way I was when I had it over a year ago.  Moreover, I remember this dish having been bigger in portions the first time.

    Trio of creme brulee: The trio of creme brulee was above average, but Chapeau does creme brulee much better (the custard part is tastier at Chapeau).

    As for service, here's a then-and-now comparison:
    Then (over a year ago): Servers thanked me after I completed each course and cleared the table of crumbs after almost every course.  

    Now: Servers NEVER thanked me and in some instances, spoke not a single word as they cleared our plates.

    Then: Staff graciously opened the door to the bathroom for me.

    Now: Not only was there no server to open the door, but the bathroom floor was also sticky.

    And most noticeable of all:
    The CHEESE CART:
    Then: I was allowed to choose around 7 different cheeses from the cheese cart.

    Now: Guests are allowed to choose only 4 cheeses.

    Gary Danko has lackluster service, the food quality has dropped, and is becoming cheap with the portions (e.g., cheese cart).  I don't think I'll be making another visit.
