    1.      lobster bisque poured over an island of baby pea puree, in a disc, topped with fried herbs, and then under the disc of peas was what I think was a liquor-gel disc, an aspic of brandy or something, so that when they poured the bisque in it melted and released the alcohol.  EXCELLENT.  this dish definitely did WOW.
    2.      glazed oysters w/caviar and zucchini pearls in a lettuce cream sauce.  very yummy but the caviar made it too salty for me.  my limited experience of good caviar is that it shouldn't be salty, but more buttery.  I'm nitpicking.  my sister loved it.  
    3.      roast maine lobster with morels, asparagus & tarragon.  another TOTAL WOW - the tarragon went perfectly with the huge chunks of lobster, sitting on a heap of creamy mashed potatoes.
    4.      roast loin of bison with green spaetzle. to me the bison was so lean it was almost flavorless, tho very tender.  but again, my sister loved it , so what do i know.
    5.      we both ordered from the cheese cart instead of getting dessert (but she got the mini dessert bday plate) as our last course -  we had 8 cheeses between us, good portions.  really really AMAZING AMAZING cheese. why, you ask?  mostly there was such a huge choice, such a range, and again, great portions.

    my 4 course menu:

    1.  foie gras.  natch.  with rhubarb. delicious, great, but I should have tried something new and different - I always get foie gras - yawn, can't believe I'm so OVER it.  geese everywhere can breath a sigh of relief.
    2.  white & green asparagus salad w/duck prosciutto.  just ok.  the prosciutto wasn't as flavorful as I expected it to be.
    3.  lemon duck breast w/duck hash & rhubarb compote.  really good, but the best thing was an incredible dollop of carrot puree with cardamom and cumin - to die for.  the duck was tender and rare and juicy.  almost as much of a wow as my sister's lobster dishes.
    4. aforementioned amazing cheese plate.

    I ordered a bottle of wine after a serious consult with the sommelier, who recommended an Austrian white - it was perfect, dry yet a touch of fruit and spice, went perfectly with all the food.  only thing was it was $80 - I told her I wanted to spend no more than $55.  she checked with me first but by that point I had already tasted it and was in love with it, so what was I going to do?  ended up spending much more than if I had done the wine pairing with each course ($65), but I got a really good wine.  don't know if it was worth $80 but it was delish.  all the wines are really expensive.  most are in the $100 range, and up.  only a few were under $60.

    oh and when we first sat down they brought us an amuse-bouche, complimentary - a little cup of vichyssoise w/tarragon oil and fried herbs.  THAT was another wow.  plus they gave us truffles and a pineapple muffin thing when we left!

    ok, as I contemplate which Lean Cuisine to pop in the microwave, i must admit this is an excellent restaurant.
