I can see why people feel that Gary Danko is a 5 star experience. In many senses it is indeed just that, but to be perfectly honest, it truly wasn't worth the money paid. They parade in front of you like a cast of characters in a play, each activity being performed by a different individual like a band of actors playing different roles. The only real "WOW factor" moment was the desert were they had a crepe with Bing cherries that were flambeed table-side. This was an additional fee on top of the bank-breaker that was the price of the 5-course dining experience. Speaking of this alleged "5-Course Experience" here is something I notices about Gary Danko. Depending on whether you selected 4 courses or 5 courses, they change the quantity of the food that is served. Thereby you are not getting more food, you are just getting the same amount of food with more courses. Therefore, with people with a heavy appetite there is no point to go with a 5 course meal, because the quantities are adjusted to span across all your meals. Therefore you are paying more money for the same amount of food. Just a heads up guys. I suppose everyone will want to go to Danko at least once in their life, but all I'm saying is for the amount of money you pay for your food, it is not justifiable for more than one visit. I would much rather spend the half the money that I would have spent at Dankos at a place that has even better food like Rivoli in Berkeley.
