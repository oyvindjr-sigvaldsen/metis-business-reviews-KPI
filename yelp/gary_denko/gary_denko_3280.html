That's part of the fun in dining out, not knowing if that cheap taqueria, or elusive hidden sushi place, or that just opened italian restaurant will ultimately be another spot checked off on the list, or, a memory that will linger forever. And when the latter occurs, you find yourself never forgetting the food that captivated with every bite, the service that was warm and inviting, the decor and ambiance that stimulated the senses and the bond that deepened with those you dined with. Gary Danko was the latter, something greater than just dinner. As the years go on and number of restaurants sampled increases, it gets harder and harder to say it was the "best ever" or "top 5 of all time". But as the recent past goes, this is right up there. An experience that has left me buzzing two days later, where I want to seize the BART loudspeaker on the way to work to sing Gary Danko's praises (not appropriate) or grab everyone I know and tell them how amazing it is (better, but still probably annoying). So instead, I'll Yelp. The minute you enter you are welcomed into a place of confidence. A confidence that they indeed deserve all the accolades received, and are thoroughly prepared to delivery on the resulting high expectations. Everything - the greeting by the hostess, the taking of our bags and jackets, the seating at our table, the explanation of the menu, the wine recommendations, the entree recommendations, the delivery of our food, the cleaning and tidying before the next dish is served - was exceptional. Between the two of us, we had the lobster risotto, pancetta wrapped frog legs, bison, stuffed quail, roast lobster and a souffle (which featured chocolate sauce being poured into the hole they created at the top - it was awesome). Finally, off the menu but just as important, we were there for three hours but it felt like three minutes. Conversation among best friends that goes back decades, with laughter towards the past and optimism towards the future, speaking in ways only people who have known each other a better part of two decades can do. And our mood was enlivened by those around us, as every single person looked like they were thoroughly enjoying themselves. I write this with a broad smile, already fondly reflecting on all that transpired. An experience where you cannot help but feel fortunate that all of the little decisions in life has ultimately led you to this moment in time, and you wouldn't want it any other way. I cannot recommend Gary Danko enough, and highly encourage you to save it for a special occasion, a special time, a special person. You won't regret it, and likely will never forget it.

At that point the host takes his offer back, and asks us to remain at the bar until my wife and I are ready to move to our table. It was a little awkward: since tables for three were available, what is the big deal in having our friend join us until he's ready to leave? We didn't want to argue and went with it. Given that we had a late reservation (9:30PM), we decided to leave our friend at the bar, and get started with dinner. At that point the host took us to our table, which turned out to be literally a couple of feet from the bar, and made it even more puzzling why he couldn't just give us a table for three in the first place!

He sat next to us on the bench (there was tons of space), we had a laugh and had him taste a bite or two of our food. Literally 60 seconds later, that same host rushes to our table, interrupts us in a rather panicked way, and asks whether we all wanted to move to the bar! Seriously!? We calmed the host down and told him that our friend was only telling us good bye, and that he'll be leaving shortly. My wife was pissed as it was a little embarrassing. Once our friend had left, we decided that I go talk to the host, which I did. He coldly explained to me that "statistically", other tables complain when folks go back and forth between the table and the bar. He didn't have any consideration to the fact that it was a special occasion, and that the entire point for going to this restaurant was for people to have fun and have a great experience. In fact, when one pays this much for a meal, isn't a big part of it the feeling of being treated specially - especially when one is celebrating a special birthday? At $500/couple the experience ought to be everything - if not, what's the point?

    Generally, I felt the restaurant staff had their mix wrong - displaying the worst formality traits (pretentious, cold, and self-rightous), and too casual when it mattered - devaluing you as a guest, and devaluing the experience.     You never know when you are about to have one of the best culinary experiences of your life. That's part of the fun in dining out, not knowing if that cheap taqueria, or elusive hidden sushi place, or that just opened italian restaurant will ultimately be another spot checked off on the list, or, a memory that will linger forever. And when the latter occurs, you find yourself never forgetting the food that captivated with every bite, the service that was warm and inviting, the decor and ambiance that stimulated the senses and the bond that deepened with those you dined with.

    Gary Danko was the latter, something greater than just dinner. As the years go on and number of restaurants sampled increases, it gets harder and harder to say it was the "best ever" or "top 5 of all time". But as the recent past goes, this is right up there. An experience that has left me buzzing two days later, where I want to seize the BART loudspeaker on the way to work to sing Gary Danko's praises (not appropriate) or grab everyone I know and tell them how amazing it is (better, but still probably annoying). So instead, I'll Yelp.

    The minute you enter you are welcomed into a place of confidence. A confidence that they indeed deserve all the accolades received, and are thoroughly prepared to delivery on the resulting high expectations. Everything - the greeting by the hostess, the taking of our bags and jackets, the seating at our table, the explanation of the menu, the wine recommendations, the entree recommendations, the delivery of our food, the cleaning and tidying before the next dish is served - was exceptional.

    As was the food. Between the two of us, we had the lobster risotto, pancetta wrapped frog legs, bison, stuffed quail, roast lobster and a souffle (which featured chocolate sauce being poured into the hole they created at the top - it was awesome). Across the board each dish was interrupted multiple times by comments such as "amazing", "best ever" and "incredible". Each dish oozed perfection, with an abundance of flavor and impeccable presentation. And quite contrary to my Orson experience, the portions were more than satisfying, and both of us felt full and satisfied with the three course option, which I felt was priced very reasonably

    And little things happened all night that made the experience better. Incredible waiters, just experts in their craft. Our half bottle of french wine was superb, and I'll never forget my friend telling me 2005 was a very good year. Indeed. We were celebrating his birthday, and in honor of the occasion they brought out a complementary plate of cookies with "Happy Birthday" spelled out on the plate in chocolate. Unexpected, yet so appreciated. We even got a little pineapple bread as a parting gift, where as my friend remarked this could be the breakfast I had wished for earlier,  when I said I never wanted to leave, but rather preferred to find a spot to nap till a morning meal.

    Finally, off the menu but just as important, we were there for three hours but it felt like three minutes. Conversation among best friends that goes back decades, with laughter towards the past and optimism towards the future, speaking in ways only people who have known each other a better part of two decades can do. And our mood was enlivened by those around us, as every single person looked like they were thoroughly enjoying themselves.

    I write this with a broad smile, already fondly reflecting on all that transpired. An experience where you cannot help but feel fortunate that all of the little decisions in life has ultimately led you to this moment in time, and you wouldn't want it any other way.

    I cannot recommend Gary Danko enough, and highly encourage you to save it for a special occasion, a special time, a special person. You won't regret it, and likely will never forget it.
