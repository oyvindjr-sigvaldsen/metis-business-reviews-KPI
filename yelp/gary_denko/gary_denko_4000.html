I loved this place. I won't go into how good the food is - the other reviews have already gone over that. My thing to add is that the 3-course meal option is really good value because it's only around $60. Most other places comparable to Gary Danko have 10-course prix fix meals at ~$120, double the price, and several of the courses are "second winds" like sherbert or fruits/cheese. And often at other places, expensive ingredients such as foie gras and truffle are offered as optional choices only, in which you have to pay extra. At Gary Dankos, you can order the traditional favorites within the $60 menu.. for example, I had (1) seared foie gras (2) lobster (3) lamb/bison. The meal comes with bread, chocolates, and cake to take home, so it's as if you've had a multiple-course meal. The only down side is that you don't get desert if you order 3 savory dishes. So for desert, you probably want the 4 course meal, which is $20 more. And the desserts look very elaborate and very good and sort of complete the dining experience if you are going there for the "experience." But as for me, I decided not to get dessert since the $20 I save is going towards my next seared foie gras at GD.
